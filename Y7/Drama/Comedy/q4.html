Question 4

Define the following comedy-specific terms and how they help to create comedy:

Satire

Satire is a literary technique that uses irony, exaggeration, humour and sarcasm to insult and expose flaws and inferior traits of individuals, or even societies as a whole. It often aims to provoke and effect change through humour.

Slapstick

Slapstick is a form of comedy typically involving the use of exaggerated physicality, ebullient actions and ridiculous situations to provoke laughter through bodily humour and physical mishaps. It leans more towards acting physically and visually rather than wit or dialogue.

Overstatement:

Also known as hyperbole, this involves exaggeration in emotions, actions or circumstances that would not be considered realistic up on the stage. This increases the intensity of the scene to get a solid reaction from the audience.

Understatement:

This involves portraying emotions or actions in a hushed, restrained manner to possibly put to effect tension and subtlety. For example, if a character is extremely happy, they may just say, “Cool,” as an understatement.

Parody

This element involves warping parts of the original work to create humour or criticising an original work itself. In short, it is a humorous or mocking imitation of something.

Imitation

Imitation can involve actors using certain character traits such as the accents, speaking patterns and behavioural patterns to bring action up onto the stage. This form of drama also provokes the audience to let go of their doubts or disbelief and allow themselves to be fully preoccupied in the world of acting.

Body language

Body language is the art of using facial expressions and body movement to convey certain emotions such as happiness, sadness, frustrated ness etc. Some stances may show a sense of confidence, possibly even with music in the background to press the point further. Every subtle detail gives the audience a deeper understanding and connection to the characters. This is a powerful tool that brings characters to life and engages the audience on an emotional level.
