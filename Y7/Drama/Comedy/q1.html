Question 1

Find out three interesting facts about the use of comedy in each of the following theatre styles:

  • Greek Theatre
  • Elizabethan Theatre
  • Commedia dell'Arte
  • Physical Theatre

Greek theatre

Greek theatre first made its way into the world of entertainment between the 6th and 4th centuries BCE. It laid the structure for lots of Western drama and theatrical conventions. Here are some facts about Greek theatre:

  • Greek theatre was formed from religious celebrations worshipping Dionysus, also known as the god of wine, fertility and ecstacy. The earliest forms of Greek theatre were these parts of the festivals.
  • The Greeks built colossal al fresco ampitheatres for their drama. The most famous one was the “Theatre of Dionysus” in Athens, which can host approximately 170,000 at once.
  • Greek actors would wear masks to represent their characteristics and emotions. As well as to magnify their voices on stage. Their full outfits included colourful garments called “Chitons”.

Elizabethan Theatre

  • The Elizabethan Theatre introduced open-air theatres, with no roof to provide natural light in the performance.
  • This era also introduced famous writers such as Shakespeare.
  • Women weren’t allowed to act, so men played all the roles including female roles.

Commedia dell-Arte

  • These plays are made without a script. Instead they have a general storyline which actors improvise throughout the play.
  • The plays featured characters that would appear through many different plays. The audience would remember them from the last play they saw.
  • These actors used exaggerated facial expressions so it was easy for the audience to recognise their emotions.

Physical theatre

  • This form heavily relies on physical features such as dance, miming and acrobatics
  • It focuses on visual expression, gestures, facial expressions.
  • They may also use themselves as a prop, creating simple movements that mean more than words.
